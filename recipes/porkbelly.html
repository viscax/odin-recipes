Chinese-Style Braised Pork belly

Description

As a kid, I would often be left in awe at the incredible smells emnating out of my kitchen. My mom has devoted herself to cooking the tastiest Chinese-Style meals from recipes passed down from generation to generation. Now, I will proudly carry on this tradition of crafting tasty, traditional meals. This recipe of mine will teach you how to make Chinese-Style Braised Pork belly (aka Hong Shao Rou in Chinese). A lengthy simmer of the porkbelly in delicious soy-sauce based soup will leave you speechless! Served with green beans immersed in the soup and on rice, Chinese-Style Braised Porkbelly is an easy recipe that won't fail to make your mouth water.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb porkbelly
  • 1 lb of skin-on pork belly
  • 1 tablespoons oil
  • 1-2 tablespoon rock sugar
  • 3 tablespoons Shaoxing Cooking Wine
  • 2-3 cups water
  • 1/2 ginger root (peeled/cut)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1/2-1 tablespoon dark soy sauce
  • 1-2 scallions

Steps

  1. Wash and chop the pork belly into 3/4 inch cubes.
  2. In the wok, boil enough water such that the pork belly can be fully submerged. Add some 1 tablespoon of the Shao Xing Cooking Wine into the water and blanch the pork in the water for upwards to 2 and a half minutes or so.
  3. Remove the pork and discard the boiled water.
  4. In the same wok on medium heat, add 1 tablespoon of oil and rock sugar. Once the oil is hot enough, place the pork belly in the wok and cook until it reaches a slightly golden color.
  5. Add water, soy sauce(s), chopped scallion, and Cooking Wine into the wok on high heat. Mix well and allow contents to boil.
  6. Turn the heat down to medium-low and cook the pork belly on a simmer for 60-75 minutes.
  7. After this, if you see that the water hasn't boiled away that much, its okay. Turn the heat to medium-high and begin to boil off the water. This way, we can turn it into a delicious sauce.
  8. At this stage, I typically add some green beans or even tofu into the wok. These ingredients will absorb the tasty flavors that came from the pork belly.
  9. When you see that the water has mostly boiled away and you are left with a delicious looking glazed sauce, the pork is ready to be served.
  10. Serve with some steamed rice and enjoy!
